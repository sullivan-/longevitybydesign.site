About

John Sullivan is a Scala Architect with over 15 years professional experience developing enterprise applications. A seasoned software engineer with a focus on back end development and database integration, he is a strong advocate of the Domain Driven Design approach to software development. John is a team player who loves to get involved in agile process. He enjoys working on build and deploy systems, performance tuning, and writing unit, integration, and acceptance tests.

John is the creator and primary author of longevity, a persistence framework for Scala and NoSQL with a Domain Driven Design orientation. When he’s not coding, he loves posting in-depth articles on his Scala-oriented blog scabl.blogspot.com. John has a Master of Science in Computer Science from UMass Boston.


Shu Li is a Big Data Solution Architect with 17 years experience in software engineering, data modeling, and database management. Her current focus is working with Big Data systems. She specializes in understanding and guiding data flow at the organizational level, and standardizing enterprise data formats.

Shu has five years experience working with Hadoop systems, including tools such as Hive, HBase, Kafka, Storm, Pig, Flume, Hue, and Sqoop. She designs warehouse systems with Redshift, and data mining pipelines with Spark. She has extensive experience with cloud platforms, including four years building AWS clusters.

Shu has a Master of Engineering in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Purdue University.
